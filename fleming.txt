                    <h1 class="section-title">What is Artificial Intelligence?</h1>
                    <p class="section-content">
                        Artificial intelligence (AI) is a set of technologies that enable computers to perform a variety of advanced functions, including the ability to see, understand and translate spoken and written language, analyze data, make recommendations, and more.

Because of this, AI is seen as a major achievement in human progress, helping society discover new ways to solve problems and create innovations.
                    </p>

                <div class="content-section" id="history">
                    <h1 class="section-title">History of AI</h1>
                    
                    <h2 class="subsection-title">Birth of Artificial Intelligence</h2>
                    <p class="section-content">
                        Artificial Intelligence, often abbreviated as "AI," refers to computer systems and machines that are built to mimic human intelligence. The concept of artificial intelligence has deep historical roots, dating back to ancient philosophers who pondered the nature of life and consciousness. In those early times, inventors created devices known as "automatons"‚Äîmechanical creations that could move on their own without human control.
                    </p>
                    <p class="section-content">
                        AI began in 1950 when British mathematician and computer science pioneer Alan Turing posed the groundbreaking question: "Can machines think?" In his influential paper titled "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," Turing introduced what is now known as the Turing Test. The term artificial intelligence was coined by McCarthy in 1956 in connection with a proposed summer workshop at Dartmouth College.
                    </p>

                    <h2 class="subsection-title">First AI Winter</h2>
                    <p class="section-content">
                        The period between the coining of the term "artificial intelligence" and the 1980s was marked by both significant progress and notable challenges in AI research. In 1960, John McCarthy developed LISP, a programming language based on the mathematical concept of recursive functions. Despite early breakthroughs, the field of AI faced a setback when an applied mathematician gave a report that led to less support and funding from the British government for the study of AI.
                    </p>

                    <h2 class="subsection-title">Expert Systems and Second AI Winter</h2>
                    <p class="section-content">
                        After the first AI Winter, it was followed by the Expert Systems era. During this time, systems that allowed computers to learn from their mistakes and make independent decisions became popular. The first expert system was released into the commercial market. However, the second AI Winter eventually arrived‚Äîa time marked by declining interest and reduced funding for AI research.
                    </p>

                    <h2 class="subsection-title">AI Agents and Artificial General Intelligence</h2>
                    <p class="section-content">
                        Following the rise of machine learning, we entered a new phase in AI development marked by the growing presence of everyday AI tools like virtual assistants and advanced search engines. In 2016, Hanson Robotics introduced Sophia, a humanoid robot with lifelike features. In 2020, OpenAI launched beta testing for GPT-3. In 2021, OpenAI released DALL¬∑E, an AI system that could generate original images based on text prompts.
                    </p>
                </div>

        function formatTime(date) {
            const now = new Date();
            const diff = Math.floor((now - date) / 1000);

            if (diff < 60) return 'Just now';
            if (diff < 3600) return Math.floor(diff / 60) + 'm ago';
            if (diff < 86400) return Math.floor(diff / 3600) + 'h ago';
            return Math.floor(diff / 86400) + 'd ago';
        }

        function loadProfiles() {
            const profilesGrid = document.getElementById('profilesGrid');
            if (profilesGrid.children.length > 0) return;
            
            profiles.forEach(profile => {
                const profileDiv = document.createElement('div');
                profileDiv.className = 'profile-circle';
                const initials = profile.name.substring(0, 1);
                profileDiv.innerHTML = `
                    <div class="circle">${initials}</div>
                    <div class="profile-name">${profile.name}</div>
                `;
                profileDiv.onclick = () => openModal(profile);
                profilesGrid.appendChild(profileDiv);
            });
        }

        function openModal(profile) {
            const initials = profile.name.substring(0, 1);
            document.getElementById('modalName').textContent = profile.name;
            document.getElementById('modalGrade').textContent = profile.grade;
            document.getElementById('modalMotto').textContent = profile.motto || '';
            document.getElementById('modalAbout').textContent = profile.about;
            document.getElementById('modalCircle').textContent = initials;
            document.getElementById('profileModal').classList.add('active');
        }

        function closeModal() {
            document.getElementById('profileModal').classList.remove('active');
        }

        document.addEventListener('click', function(e) {
            const modal = document.getElementById('profileModal');
            if (e.target === modal) {
                closeModal();
            }
        });
